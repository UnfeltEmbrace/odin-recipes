Fried Rice


Description

This is a recipe for a simple fried rice that's down to its main components for those who are in a budget.

Ingredients

  • Chopped up Garlic
  • Day-old rice - It's best if you use leftover rice than a freshly cooked one
  • Salt
  • High smoking point oil (e.g. Canola, Grapeseed, Peanut, etc.)

Steps

  1. Heat up the pan on the highest setting until you see small streaks of smoke.
  2. When you see the small streaks of smoke, add some oil to the pan and swirl it around to oil up the pan's inner surface and wait for light smoke.
  3. Once you see the oil smoking, discard it to somewhere like, a ceramic bowl for later use to make the fried rice not dry.
  4. Add a fresh batch of oil and once you see it shimmer, turn the heat down to medium and add the garlic.
  5. Saute the garlic until you can smell the aroma, then add the rice and put the heat back to the highest setting.
  6. Stirr it up around, occasionally bashing clumps to unclump it, then add salt.
  7. Serve.
